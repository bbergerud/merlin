Adult male. Small streamlined songbird with a tiny bill, long, pointed wings and a short, squared or slightly notched tail. Adult males are blue-green above and white below with blackish flight feathers. 
Adult female. Long-bodied songbird with a small head and bill. Adult females are brownish above with hints of blue-green though some females can be nearly as blue-green as males.

Adult male. In flight, note streamlined body, squared or slightly notched tail and broad wings. Adult males are blue-green above with a black mask around their eye and tiny white crescents on the sides of their rump. 
Juvenile. The wings are broad and the tail is squared or slightly notched. Juveniles are brown above with a small white crescent on the sides of its rump.

Adult male. Adult males are iridescent blue-green with a thin black mask around the eye. 
Juvenile. Juveniles are brown above and white below, but some juveniles and females can show a weak, blurry gray-brown breast band. 
Immature. 
Immature. Immature birds are brown above with patches of adult feathers growing in on their backs. 
Adult male. Breeds in open habitats such as fields and wetlands, usually adjacent to water. They nest in artificial nest boxes as well as holes in trees.
Flock. 
Flock. Forms large flocks during migration and when foraging over wetlands, water, and agricultural fields.
