Adult. Larger oriole with a straight bill. Males and females look similar, though females tend to be paler. Adults have a black head with lemon yellow bellies and yellow-olive backs. 
Immature. Immature birds may not have a completely black head.

Adult. Larger oriole with a long, straight bill. Adults have a black tail, head, and wings and are yellow below and yellow-olive above. 
Immature. Predominantly found in Mexico. In the U.S. only found in southern Texas. Rather secretive oriole, living in denser vegetation than most other orioles and singing from inconspicuous perches.
