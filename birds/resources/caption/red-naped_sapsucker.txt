Male. Small woodpecker with vertical white patch on the folded wing. Males have a red throat, crown, and small nape patch. 
Female. Females have a small white chin patch under the bill, while males have a fully red chin and throat. The nape on females can be red or white. 
Male. Note the red sides of the throat without a black border (Yellow-bellied Sapsuckers have a full black border to throat). Males have a fully red chin and throat; females have a white chin patch. 
Juvenile. Juveniles are washed in brown with a brown crown. 
Male. Drills rows of parallel circular holes known as sap wells in tree bark to access tree sap. 
Male. Breeds in mixed forests of willow, aspen, birch, ponderosa pine, juniper, or Douglas-fir.
