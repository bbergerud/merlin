Adult. Large, flat-headed nightjar. Upperparts are mottled brown, buff, and black. In flight, males flash thin stripes of white on the tail. Males and females do not flash white in the wing. 
Adult. Extremely well camouflaged. Often roosts on the ground or tree branch during the day.
Adult. Roosts during the day on a branch or on the ground. Tends to roost in the same location night after night. 
Adult female. Flat-headed nightjar with a long tail and long, pointed wings. Females have a buffy spot on the tips of the outer tail feathers. 
Adult. Incessantly sings its name at night.
