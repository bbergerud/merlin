Breeding male. Breeding males have a deep brownish-red body with a long black bill, red eye, and a black back and rear.
Female/nonbreeding male. Females and nonbreeding males have a coarsely marked brown body with faint darker eyeline and cap, and a long black bill.
Typically found in shallow freshwater wetlands such as marshes, flooded fields and ditches, ponds, and slow streams.
Breeding male. In flight note the green secondaries and powdery-blue shoulder.
Breeding male and female. Note the plain-faced look on the female and the bright cinnamon-colored male. 
Breeding male. In flight shows bright white underwings.
Breeding male with Blue-winged Teal. Often flocks with other duck species, including Blue-winged Teal.
