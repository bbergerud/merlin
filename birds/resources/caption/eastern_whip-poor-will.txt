Adult. Round-headed nightjar with a stout chest. Patterned with a complicated mottling of gray and brown, which provides good camouflage. Note gray line of feathers on the back. 
Adult. Some individuals are more rusty colored than others. 
Adult. Active during the night; typically sleeps during the day, when its camouflaged plumage can make it very hard to spot. 
Adult. Round-headed nightjar with line of gray feathers on the back. Females have small buffy tail corners and males have large white tail corners, visible in flight. 
Male. Calls at night, a nearly endless chanting "whip-poor-will." Males have large white flashes in the tail.
