Male. Barred grayish brown-and-white chickenlike grouse with a short tail. Males raise the feathers on the side of their neck to expose bare patches of reddish skin, which they inflate to produce booming sounds. 
Female. Barred grayish brown-and-white chickenlike grouse with a short tail and small head. Females have a pale throat and eyeline. 
Juvenile. Barred grayish brown-and-white chickenlike grouse. Juveniles look like females with a pale throat and eyeline. 
Male. Found in native prairies.
