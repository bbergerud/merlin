Adult male. Long-tailed nightjar. Male's outer tail feathers are white. They are reduced on females, but noticeable in flight. Wings also have a white band that is visible in flight. 
Adult. Well-camouflaged, brown and gray nightjar. Feathers on the shoulder have buffy tips. Individuals in Texas and lower Rio Grande Valley are grayer than others.
Adult. Forages at dawn and dusk near clearings for insects. Rests on the ground and becomes "immobilized" in spotlights.
